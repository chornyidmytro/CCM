Brief view of CCM history

July 1954
Tanganyika African National Union (TANU) was formed from the Tanganyika African Association by Julius Nyerere, when he was teaching at St. Francis' College (which is now known as Pugu High School).

February 1957
Establishment of the Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP), which was a Marxist-Leninist, African nationalist Zanzibari political party, in future headed by Abeid Karume to begin the Zanzibar Revolution on 12 January, 1964.

February 1977
The CCM party was created under the leadership of Julius Nyerere, through the merger of the Tanganyika African National Union (TANU), the ruling party in Tanganyika, and the Afro-Shirazi Party (ASP), the ruling party in Zanzibar.

August 1990
Julius Nyerere retired CCM leadership and picked Ali Hassan Mwinyi to be his successor.

July 2015
John Magufuli was nominated as CCM's presidential candidate for the 2015 election, winning the majority votes against his opponents and later he also won a national elections.
